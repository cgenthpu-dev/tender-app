SECTION II: TERMS & CONDITIONS OF CONTRACT (ATC)

1. General Conditions

1.1. Scope of Work: The scope shall include Supply, Installation, Commissioning, & Satisfactory Demonstration of the Vector Network Analyzer. This will also include testing, packing, transportation, transit insurance, delivery at the site, unloading, and any other services associated with the delivery of the equipment. The successful bidder will assume full responsibility for the complete system until final acceptance.

1.2. No Gratification Clause: The bidder must provide an undertaking that they will not attempt to gratify any person or use any unfair means to influence the purchase process. Any such action will debar the company from participating in this and future tenders, and suitable action will be initiated.



1.3. No Cartel Formation: Bidders shall not form a cartel or submit supporting quotations for other companies. Evidence of such practices will lead to disqualification.

1.4. Purchaser's Right to Vary Quantities: The Purchaser reserves the right to increase or decrease the quantity to be ordered at the time of placement of the contract. The purchaser also reserves the right to place a repeat order during the currency of the contract at the contracted rates. Bidders are bound to accept the orders accordingly.

1.5. Purchaser’s Right to Accept or Reject Bids: The Purchaser reserves the right to accept or reject any bid, and to annul the bidding process and reject all bids at any time prior to the award of contract, without thereby incurring any liability to the affected bidder(s).

1.6. Patents, Copyrights, and Indemnification: The successful bidder shall protect and fully indemnify the Purchaser from any claims for infringement of patents, copyright, trademark, or the like. The successful bidder shall also be responsible for compliance with all legal requirements and shall indemnify the Purchaser from any claims or penalties arising out of any infringements.

1.7. Restrictions on Procurement: Any bidder from country which shares a land border with India will be eligible to bid in this procurement process if the bidder is registered with competent authority constituted by Department for promotion of industry and internal trade (DPIIT) or any other body as created by Govt. of India/Ministry of Finance, Department of Expenditure, Public Procurement Division New Delhi.

Bidder from a country which shares a land border with India for purpose of this bid means: -

An entity incorporated, established or registered in such a country; or  

A subsidiary of an entity incorporated, established or registered in such a country; or  

An entity substantially controlled through entities incorporated, established or registered in such a country; or  

An entity whose beneficial owner is situated in such a country; or

An Indian (or other) agent of such entity; or 

A natural person who is citizen of such country; or

A consortium or joint venture where any member of the consortium or joint venture falls under any above.

Bidder Status 

The bidder shall be an OEM Manufacturer, Direct Importer, or Authorized Dealer/Distributor of the OEM. Authorized Dealers/Distributors must submit a current, bid-specific Manufacturer’s Authorization Form (MAF).



Experience

Supporting documents shall include Purchase Orders and corresponding Consignee Receipt and Acceptance Certificates (CRACs) for last three financial years i.e. 2022-2023, 2023-2024, 2024-2025.

Forfeiture of EMD The EMD shall be liable to forfeiture under the following conditions:

If any document submitted is found to be false or misleading.

Bid Rejection Bids submitted without valid EMD shall be summarily rejected.



4. Performance Security

Requirement: The successful bidder shall be required to furnish a Performance Bank Guarantee (PBG) i.e. 10% of the total contract value. This guarantee shall be submitted within 10 days from the date of the issuance of the Letter of Intent (LOI)/award of contract.



Form of Guarantee: The PBG shall be in the form of an unconditional and irrevocable FDR or bank guarantee issued by a Nationalized Bank in favour of Finance Officer, Himachal Pradesh University, Summer Hill, Shimla. The PBG shall remain valid for a period of at least two months beyond the completion of the contract period and or warranty period.



Purpose: The PBG shall serve as security for the due performance of the contract and shall cover any failure or default in the execution of the contract by the successful bidder.



Forfeiture: In the event of any breach of contract, failure to perform the obligations as per the terms of the contract, or failure to deliver the services/materials within the stipulated time frame, the PBG shall be forfeited in part or in full, as determined by Himachal Pradesh University.



Release of Guarantee: The PBG shall be released after the successful completion of the contract and upon submission of a written request by the contractor, along with a certificate of satisfactory performance from the Chairperson, Department of Physics, Himachal Pradesh University.



Non-Compliance: Failure to submit the PBG within the stipulated time by the L1 bidder shall result in the cancellation of the contract and forfeiture of the Earnest Money Deposit (EMD).

6.3. Bidders who qualify the technical evaluation will be considered for the financial bid opening.

6.4. The contract will be awarded to the bidder who is technically qualified and quotes the lowest price (L1) as per GeM evaluation rules.

8.2. The quoted price must be all-inclusive (FOR destination) and cover all costs such as basic price, packing, forwarding, freight, transit insurance, all applicable taxes (GST), duties, handling, loading/unloading, and any other charges for delivery and installation at the Department of Physics, HPU, Shimla.

8.3. No price variation, escalation, or additional claims on account of market fluctuations will be permitted after the submission of the bid.



9. Delivery, Installation, and Commissioning

9.1. Timeline: The equipment must be delivered, installed, tested, and commissioned within 60 days from the date of issue of the supply order.

9.2. Late Delivery/Installation Penalty: A penalty of 0.5% of the total contract value per week of delay will be imposed for delays in delivery/installation, up to a maximum of 10% of the contract value.

9.3. Failure to Deliver: If the delay exceeds the period for which the maximum penalty is applicable (20 weeks), the University reserves the right to cancel the purchase order, forfeit the Performance Security, and blacklist the firm.

9.4. Training: The supplier shall be responsible for providing comprehensive training on the operation, maintenance, and basic troubleshooting of the equipment to faculty/staff at no extra cost.

9.5. Site Preparation: The Purchaser shall provide the site for installation as per the environmental and electrical specifications prescribed by the supplier. It is the supplier's responsibility to provide these site requirements in advance.

10. Inspection and Tests

10.1. The Purchaser or its representative shall have the right to inspect and/or test the equipment to confirm its conformity to the contract specifications at no extra cost to the Purchaser. The Purchaser may also opt for a Third-Party Inspection (TPI) at its own discretion.

10.2. On Inspection, should any inspected or tested equipment fail to conform to the specifications, the Purchaser may reject the equipment, and the supplier shall either replace the rejected equipment or make necessary alterations to meet specification requirements free of cost.

10.3. For the purpose of taking over the equipment, a final acceptance test shall be carried out at the Purchaser's site. Only the equipment that meets all acceptance test criteria shall be accepted.



11. Technical Documentation and Manuals

11.1. The bidder shall supply one complete set of technical documentation with the equipment, which must include detailed Operations, Installation, and Maintenance manuals in English. The manuals should be comprehensive enough to provide clear guidance to maintenance personnel.

12. Warranty

12.1. The equipment, including all parts and accessories, must be covered under a comprehensive on-site warranty for a minimum period of 3 years from the date of successful installation and commissioning.

12.2. Warranty charges shall not be quoted separately; they must be included in the price of the equipment. Offers with separate warranty charges will be summarily rejected.

12.3. If the performance of the equipment is not satisfactory during the warranty period, it shall be replaced by the supplier free of cost. The warranty period for the replaced parts/equipment shall be extended for a period equal to the downtime.



13. Technical Assistance and Maintenance (After-Sales Service)

13.1. During the warranty period, the bidder must provide technical assistance/maintenance for the product within 48 hours of a ticket being raised by the department. The firm must provide an uptime guarantee of 95%.

13.2. Failure to provide service within the stipulated time will attract a penalty of Rs. 5,000/- per day upto first week, and Rs. 10,000/- per day thereafter.

13.3. This penalty amount will be deducted from the Performance Security deposited by the bidder.

13.4. If a bidder fails to provide maintenance service for a continuous period of 45 days, the Performance Bank Guarantee (PBG) will be forfeited, and the firm will be blacklisted from participating in future tenders of the University.

13.5. Bidders shall furnish details of their back-up engineering and system support infrastructure available in India.



14. Spare Parts Availability

14.1.  The bidder must provide a self-declaration/undertaking that it will provide/keep spare parts for the product for at least 7 years after the completion of the warranty period.



15. Packing and Marking

15.1. All packing must be strong enough to withstand rough handling during transit. Fragile articles must be packed with special precautions and shall bear markings like 'FRAGILE', 'HANDLE WITH CARE', and 'THIS SIDE UP'.

15.2. All delicate surfaces of the equipment shall be carefully protected with appropriate materials to prevent rusting and damage. The supplier shall be liable for any damage attributable to defective or insufficient packing.



16. Substitution and Change of Model

16.1. Unauthorized substitution or materials delivered that do not comply with the specified description or quality shall be taken back by the supplier at their own cost and risk.

16.2. No change of model after the placement of the order shall be entertained unless the alternate model offered is equivalent or higher in specifications and written approval has been obtained from the Purchaser prior to delivery.



17. Payment Terms

17.1. 100% payment will be released after successful delivery, installation, commissioning, and acceptance of the equipment by the designated committee.
