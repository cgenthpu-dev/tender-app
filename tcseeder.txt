Unified General Terms and Conditions

Category: Instructions for Bidders
  1.1 Bid Submission: Bidders must be registered on the Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal. All bids, including technical and financial components, must be submitted exclusively online through the GeM portal (gem.gov.in) before the specified deadline. Physical or offline bids will not be accepted.
  1.2 Governing Terms: This bid process will be governed by the General Terms and Conditions (GTC) of GeM in addition to the Additional Terms and Conditions (ATC) specified in this document. In case of any conflict, the terms specified in this document will prevail.
  1.3 Digital Signature: Bids must be digitally signed by an authorized representative of the bidding firm using a valid Class-II or Class-III Digital Signature Certificate (DSC).
  1.4 Documentation: Bidders are required to upload scanned copies of all necessary documents as specified in this bid document. All uploaded documents must be clear, legible, and duly signed and stamped by the authorized signatory.

  2.1 Scope of Work: The scope shall include Supply, Installation, Commissioning, & Satisfactory Demonstration of {{scope_of_work}}. This will also include testing, packing, transportation, transit insurance, delivery at the site, unloading, and any other services associated with the delivery of the equipment. The successful bidder will assume full responsibility for the complete system until final acceptance.
  2.2 No Gratification Clause: The bidder must provide an undertaking that they will not attempt to gratify any person or use any unfair means to influence the purchase process. Any such action will debar the company from participating in this and future tenders, and suitable action will be initiated.

The purchaser also reserves the right to place a repeat order during the currency of the contract at the contracted rates. Bidders are bound to accept the orders accordingly.
  2.5 Purchaser’s Right to Accept or Reject Bids: The Purchaser reserves the right to accept or reject any bid, and to annul the bidding process and reject all bids at any time prior to the award of contract, without thereby incurring any liability to the affected bidder(s).
  2.6 Patents, Copyrights, and Indemnification: The successful bidder shall protect and fully indemnify the Purchaser from any claims for infringement of patents, copyright, trademark, or the like. The successful bidder shall also be responsible for compliance with all legal requirements and shall indemnify the Purchaser from any claims or penalties arising out of any infringements.
  2.7 Restrictions on Procurement: Any bidder from country which shares a land border with India will be eligible to bid in this procurement process if the bidder is registered with competent authority constituted by Department for promotion of industry and internal trade (DPIIT) or any other body as created by Govt. of India/Ministry of Finance, Department of Expenditure, Public Procurement Division New Delhi.

  3.1 Bidder Status: The bidder shall be an OEM Manufacturer, Direct Importer, or Authorized Dealer/Distributor of the OEM. Authorized Dealers/Distributors must submit a current, bid-specific Manufacturer’s Authorization Form (MAF).
  3.2 Experience: The bidder must have a minimum of {{experience_years}} years’ experience in India in supply, installation, and commissioning of similar scientific test and measurement equipment. Supporting documents shall include Purchase Orders and corresponding Consignee Receipt and Acceptance Certificates (CRACs) for last three financial years i.e. {{financial_years}}.
  3.3 Past Performance: The Bidder or its OEM should have supplied same or similar Category Products for {{past_performance_percentage}}% of bid quantity, in at least one of the last three financial years before the bid opening date to any Central / State Govt Organization / PSU. Copies of relevant contracts (proving supply of cumulative order quantity in any one financial year) to be submitted along with bid in support of quantity supplied in the relevant financial year.
  3.4 Financial Capacity: The bidder must have a minimum average annual turnover of {{min_turnover}} over the last three financial years. The turnover must be certified by a Chartered Accountant, and supported by audited financial statements for the respective years.
  3.5 Income Tax Returns: The bidder shall submit Income Tax Returns (ITRs) statement for the financial years {{financial_years}}.
  3.6 Tax and Registration Compliance: The bidder must hold valid PAN and GST Registration, and be registered on the GeM portal.
  3.7 Blacklisting Declaration: A notarized self-declaration on non-judicial stamp paper must be submitted, confirming that the firm has not been blacklisted/debarred by any Central/State Government Department, PSU, or Government organization.
  3.8 Land-Border Compliance: Compliance with Government of India restrictions, on procurement from bidders of countries sharing land borders with India must be declared. Certificate to be submitted as per applicable format.

Category: Earnest Money Deposit (EMD)
  4.1 EMD Requirement: The bidder shall submit an Earnest Money Deposit (EMD) of {{emd_amount}} ({{emd_amount_words}}) along with the bid. The EMD shall be submitted in any of the following forms: Fixed Deposit Receipt (FDR) from a nationalized bank, duly pledged in the name of {{emd_pledge_officer}}; OR Bank Guarantee (BG) from a nationalized bank, drawn in favor of {{emd_pledge_officer}}.
  4.2 Submission of Original EMD Instrument: The original EMD instrument (FDR or BG) must be physically submitted to the office of {{submission_officer}}, {{department_address}}, before or within {{emd_submission_days}} working days after the last date of bid submission on the GeM portal. The envelope must be clearly labelled with the bid reference number, bidder name, and contact details.
  4.3 Refund of EMD: EMD of unsuccessful bidders shall be refunded after finalization of the tender and within 30 days from the award of contract to the successful bidder. EMD of the successful bidder shall be refunded upon submission of Performance Security and execution of the contract agreement. No claim for interest on EMD shall be entertained under any circumstances.
  4.4 Forfeiture of EMD: The EMD shall be liable to forfeiture under the following conditions: If the bidder withdraws or modifies the bid during the bid validity period; If the successful bidder fails to sign the contract or submit the required Performance Security within the stipulated time; If any document submitted is found to be false or misleading.
  4.5 Bid Rejection: Bids submitted without valid EMD shall be summarily rejected.

Category: Performance Security
  5.1 Requirement: The successful bidder shall be required to furnish a Performance Bank Guarantee (PBG) i.e. {{pbg_percentage}}% of the total contract value. This guarantee shall be submitted within {{pbg_submission_days}} days from the date of the issuance of the Letter of Intent (LOI)/award of contract.
  5.2 Form of Guarantee: The PBG shall be in the form of an unconditional and irrevocable FDR or bank guarantee issued by a Nationalized Bank in favour of {{emd_pledge_officer}}. The PBG shall remain valid for a period of at least {{pbg_validity_period}} months beyond the completion of the contract period and or warranty period.
  5.3 Purpose: The PBG shall serve as security for the due performance of the contract and shall cover any failure or default in the execution of the contract by the successful bidder.
  5.4 Forfeiture: In the event of any breach of contract, failure to perform the obligations as per the terms of the contract, or failure to deliver the services/materials within the stipulated time frame, the PBG shall be forfeited in part or in full, as determined by Himachal Pradesh University.
  5.5 Release of Guarantee: The PBG shall be released after the successful completion of the contract and upon submission of a written request by the contractor, along with a certificate of satisfactory performance from the Chairperson, Department of Physics, Himachal Pradesh University.
  5.6 Non-Compliance: Failure to submit the PBG within the stipulated time by the L1 bidder shall result in the cancellation of the contract and forfeiture of the Earnest Money Deposit (EMD).

  7.4 Award Criteria: The contract will be awarded to the bidder who is technically qualified and quotes the lowest price (L1) as per GeM evaluation rules.
